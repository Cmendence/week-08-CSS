Nothing quite compares to playing a video game for the first time and getting that feeling of awe as you are immersed in a new world. With technology advancing faster than ever and thousands of new games being released every year, it's easy to overlook the games that changed us. Whether it's awesome gameplay, cool new mechanics, beautiful landscapes, a gripping story, or a combination of these, gamers often wear their favorite games as a badge of honor. With that being said...

Here are my top 5 video games



  1. Portal
    I awoke in the Aperture Science Computer-Aided Enrichment Center for the first time... and immediately threw both the radio and the coffee cup into the toilet.
    Initially released as part of the Orange Box, most thought Portal was a trial. In reality, it was a phenomena; a physics game masterpiece that changed the world of puzzle games.
    You play as Chell, a test subject at Aperture Science. Led by GLaDOS (Genetic Lifeform and Disk Operating System), you take part in a series of tests - physics-bending puzzles - using the Aperture Science Handheld Portal Device. After Chell finishes the final test and is offered her reward(cake), we quickly learn GLaDOS has other plans...

    But remember! The cake is a lie! ...Or is it?



  2. Portal 2
    Just when you think perfect can't get any more perfecterer... They made a Portal 2! This game picks up after the events of the first game, and re-raises the bar of what puzzle games could be. The story of Chell, Cave Johnson, and Caroline is very easy to miss, but is so deep that you will keep finding yourself wondering for weeks afterward what could have been, or, what actually was...?

    Also, they added co-op! After the events of Portal 2, GLaDOS has now created two new reusable robots for testing. No longer is she burdened with having to find humans to test her experiments! You play as ATLAS and P-body in a whole new set of test chambers built for two people, requiring two portal guns for twice the fun!



  3. The Witness
    In The Witness, you awake in a dark chamber. After opening the door, you find yourself on a beautiful but deserted island. You do not know how you arrived here, but as you explore, you keep finding maze-type puzzle panels. Using the tools provided, you progress through the game and learn the many panel mechanics, and slowly unlock pieces of the mystery surrounding this magical place.



  4. The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild
    Sorry, Link! But our Princess is in anoth.. Oh, she is in the castle? And she's trapped! From the moment you step out of your suspended animation chamber and see the vast Kingdom of Hyrule, you'll be hooked. Fighting, cooking, flying, and saving the day! What more could you ask for? The characters are lively. The story is engaging and funny. I want to go there. But wait! What's that over there?? You can easily sink 50+ hours into this game and not finish the main story.
    Fun for all play styles, BotW is the perfect blend of puzzle game and open world RPG.



  5. Halo: Reach
    Halo: Reach is the only first-person shooter that ever made any sense to me. You play as Master Chief, a towering supersoldier known as a "Spartan", raised and trained from childhood for combat.
    Released in 2010, Halo was already a popular and established franchise. I got into Halo: Reach because some friends were into it (spoiler- we were all terrible). 13 years later, it is still my go-to game when I need to unwind and shoot some bad guys. It differs from Call of Duty type FPS games in that the Spartan soldiers run faster, jump higher, and have armor as well as health. Also, with Halo: Reach, the game introduced new abilities(called Loadouts) that enable the player to do things like create a decoy hologram, use a jet pack, and create an impenetrable shield for a short time.
